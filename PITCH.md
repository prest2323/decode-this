# Decode This — Pitch (owned by Person 4)

## One-liner
**Decode This — for the paperwork you can't read, and the words you can't find.**

## 90-second script (fill in / rehearse)
1. **The pain (15s).** "This is a real letter a Bakersfield family received." *(hold up the redacted Medi-Cal letter)* "Mom speaks Spanish. She doesn't understand it. There's a deadline she can't see."
2. **Decode, live (25s).** Photograph it → explanation appears → **tap Read Aloud → it speaks in Spanish.** *(pause — let the room hear it)* Point out the ⚠️ deadline flag and the ready-to-send reply.
3. **The twist (25s).** "Now the other direction." Switch to **Find my words** → mumble: *"mi hijo está enfermo, no puede ir a la escuela"* → out comes a polished English email to the teacher. "Same tool, both ways."
4. **Close (25s).** "Decode This decodes everything — the paperwork you can't read, and the words you can't find." *(show QR)* "Scan it — try it on something in your own pocket right now."

## What we learned (3 bullets — judging criterion)
- _e.g._ How vision models handle low-quality phone photos of real documents, and what fails.
- _e.g._ Bilingual UX: translation isn't enough — tone and "what do I do next" matter more.
- _e.g._ Designing for low-literacy/accessibility (read-aloud, big type, one action).

## Why it matters here
Built for Kern County: a community where many families navigate English-only paperwork every day.
